MSc in Computer Science

Stanford University - Advance your career in technology with a world-class computer science education

Program Overview

The Master of Science in Computer Science at Stanford University is designed for students who want to deepen their knowledge of computer science and pursue advanced research in the field. This program offers a rigorous curriculum that combines theoretical foundations with practical applications, preparing graduates for leadership roles in technology companies, research institutions, and innovative startups.

Program Highlights

  • World-renowned faculty including Turing Award winners
  • Cutting-edge research opportunities in AI, systems, and theory
  • Silicon Valley location with industry connections
  • Flexible curriculum with numerous specialization options
  • Entrepreneurship opportunities and startup support
  • Strong alumni network in top tech companies

Career Opportunities

Graduates of this program pursue careers as:

  • Software Engineers at leading tech companies
  • Research Scientists in AI and machine learning
  • Data Scientists and analysts
  • Systems architects and engineers
  • Technology entrepreneurs and startup founders
  • Academic researchers and professors

Specialization Tracks

Artificial Intelligence

Focus on machine learning, natural language processing, computer vision, and robotics.

Systems

Study operating systems, distributed systems, networking, and database systems.

Security

Learn about cryptography, network security, and secure software development.

Data Science

Focus on statistical methods, data mining, and big data analytics.

Program Curriculum

Core Courses

Required Foundation Courses

  • Advanced Algorithms
  • Computer Systems Architecture
  • Probability for Computer Scientists
  • Research Methodology in Computer Science
Elective Courses

Artificial Intelligence Track

  • Machine Learning
  • Natural Language Processing
  • Computer Vision
  • Reinforcement Learning

Systems Track

  • Distributed Systems
  • Cloud Computing
  • Database Systems Implementation
  • Network Programming
Research Component

Thesis Option

Students can choose to complete a master's thesis under faculty supervision, conducting original research in their chosen specialization.

Project Option

Alternatively, students can complete a significant project, often in collaboration with industry partners or research labs.

Admission Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or related field
  • Minimum GPA of 3.5/4.0 or equivalent
  • Strong background in mathematics and programming
  • Official academic transcripts
  • GRE scores (optional but recommended)

Application Documents

  • Completed application form
  • Statement of purpose
  • Professional resume/CV
  • Three letters of recommendation
  • Proof of English proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL)

Technical Skills

  • Proficiency in programming languages (Python, Java, C++)
  • Understanding of data structures and algorithms
  • Experience with software development
  • Knowledge of computer systems fundamentals
