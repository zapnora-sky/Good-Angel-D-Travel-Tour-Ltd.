UK Student Visa Requirements

Complete guide to obtaining your Student visa for studying in the United Kingdom

UK Student Visa Overview

The UK Student visa (formerly Tier 4 visa) allows international students to study in the United Kingdom at an accredited institution. You must have been offered a place on a course by a licensed student sponsor, have enough money to support yourself and pay for your course, and be able to speak, read, write and understand English.

Basic Eligibility Requirements

  • You must be 16 or older to apply
  • You must have an unconditional offer from a licensed student sponsor
  • You must have enough money to support yourself and pay for your course
  • You must be able to speak, read, write and understand English
  • You must have consent from your parents if you're 16 or 17

Required Documents

  • Valid passport or other travel documentation
  • Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from your course provider
  • Proof of sufficient funds to cover course fees and living costs
  • Passport-sized color photograph
  • Tuberculosis test results (if applicable)
  • Academic Technology Approval Scheme (ATAS) certificate (if required)
  • Proof of parental consent (if under 18)
  • Proof of relationship to parent or guardian (if under 18)
  • English language proficiency evidence

Financial Requirements

You must show you have enough money to support yourself and pay for your course. The amount varies depending on your circumstances:

  • £1,334 per month (for up to 9 months) for courses in London
  • £1,023 per month (for up to 9 months) for courses outside London
  • Course fees for the first year (as stated on your CAS)
  • Funds must be held for 28 consecutive days

Application Tips & Requirements

Financial Evidence Requirements

• Funds must be held for 28 consecutive days
• Bank statements must be recent (within 31 days)
• Online statements must be stamped by the bank
• Funds can be in your name or parent's/guardian's name
• If using parent's funds, include birth certificate and consent letter

English Language Requirements

You must prove your knowledge of English by:
• Passing a Secure English Language Test (SELT)
• Having a GCSE/A-level in English
• Being from an English-speaking country
• Having a degree taught in English
• Meeting the level required by your course

Working on Student Visa

• 20 hours per week during term time
• Full-time during vacations
• Work placement allowed if part of course
• Cannot be self-employed
• Cannot work as professional sportsperson
• Cannot work as doctor in training (except foundation program)

Bringing Family Members

You can bring dependents if you are:
• A government-sponsored student doing a course longer than 6 months
• Doing a postgraduate level course of 9 months or longer
• Each dependent must pay £363 application fee
• Each dependent must pay healthcare surcharge
• Must show additional funds for dependents
